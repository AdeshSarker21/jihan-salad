About Me

Jihan Salad, MSc is a Sexual and Reproductive Health specialist at UNFPA Jordan, with more than 10 years of international experience in the areas of Maternal and Reproductive Health, Youth Engagement, Gender Equality and Humanitarian Response.

After spending most of her life in the Netherlands as a Somali refugee, Jihan decided to return to Somalia at the age of 23. Despite the risks and uncertainties, Jihan was determined to fulfil her lifelong calling to serve women and girls in countries where the health challenges are the greatest.

She worked as a senior Technical Specialist for Maternal and Reproductive Health at the United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA). During her career at the Fund, Jihan Salad has worked in all major areas of Maternal and Reproductive Health, Youth Engagement, Human Rights and Gender Equality, and Population Matters.

Despite periods of conflict and insecurity in Somalia, Jihan has managed to successfully oversee the launch of multiple notable initiatives, including the Campaign on the Accelerated Reduction of Maternal Mortality in Africa (CARMMA) and the first-ever forensic centre to strengthen the medico-legal response to gender-based violence. She also supported the launch of the first-ever Women in Global Health Somalia conference.

Prior to joining UNFPA, Jihan served as Health Promotion and Disease Prevention Specialist at the International Organization for Migration and the Ministry of Health in Somalia (Puntland Regional Government).

She holds a Bachelor’s degree in Health Sciences and a Master’s degree in Prevention and Public Health from the VU University of Amsterdam, the Netherlands.

Jihan Salad has written on a range of social, humanitarian and development issues. Her articles have been featured in UNFPA website and ReliefWeb, the largest humanitarian information portal in the world. Jihan is an effective communicator with extensive networks in global health and international development.

Jihan is a passionate advocate for the 2030 Global Agenda for Sustainable Development and the International Conference on Population and Development (ICPD) Programme of Action. She is deeply committed to improving the lives and prospects of the most vulnerable.
